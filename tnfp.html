About TNFP

PREAMBLE :

Anantapur District is one of the drought – prone districts in India, where the major profession of the people is farming or farm-based employment. The major corps produced in the district are groundnut, paddy, millets and different greens and vegetables. Dryland Horticulture has been widely promoted by the Department of Horticulture keeping in view the driest conditions in the district.

PROFILE OF THE IMPLEMENTING ORGANIZATION

Tirumala Natural Food Products Joint Liability Group (TNFP JLG) is started on 22 nd January, 2014. In the presence of the District Collector Shri. Lakshmikantham, IAS garu the inauguration was attended by Mr. Sri Ramamurthy, JDA, Mr. Lakshmi Reddy, Program Co- Ordinator, KVK Reddypalli and Mr. Malla Reddy, Program Director, AF Ecology Center. The Program was Organized in the Rythu Bazar, Anantarpur. Tirumala Natural Food Products JLG started a small scale millet value-added food processing unit at 5-12-13, Anjaneya Swamy Nagar, Bellary By-pass road, Anantapur on the date of 07 th November, 2017.

AIM

Tirumala Natural Food Products JLG procure locally produced agriculture produce. Preparation and promotion of millet value-added products. To help them in getting better price for their produce. There by enhancing the economic status and health of the locally farmers, and give the healthy millet food products to the customers.

OBJECTIVE
  • To improve the income of the farmers and farmer-based workers to improvise the socio-economic conditions.
  • To create scope of self-employment by engaging un-employed youth/ local Wenders in the marketing the value-added produce.
  • To Create marketing linkages value added produce.
  • To supply the healthy millet food products to the children, women and anemia patients.
  • To supply the high-quality millet value added products to super markets, Malls, Agencies and local Wenders.
IMPORTANCE OF THE VALUE ADDITION

Anantapur district is one of the most drought-prone areas, and about 70% of agriculture come under dryland farming. The farmers have to depend on seasonal rains for the single crop they cultivate in their dry lands. However, the agricultural inputs they have to invest are the same – i.e., they have to invest on various farm works (activities like land ploughing, levelling, sowing, weeding, harvesting etc.), and spraying of fertilizers and pesticides. The risk in this farm of agriculture is the failure of crops due to failure of rains (in-time monsoonal failures). This results in heavy losses to the farmers. The major crops produced in the district are groundnut, paddy, millets and different greens and vegetables. However, in the case of a good monsoon season also the farmers are seen entering into losses due to steep downfall of market prices. As a result, the farmers are pushed to go for distress sale thereby incurring heavy losses. The farmers, in this area, sell the produce as a raw material – a process in which they are cheated on prices as well as on weights – both acts incurring loss to the farmers. Under these conditions, there is a need to see that the farmers are given a chance to either preserve the agricultural produce, or push it into the market by way of converting them into some other form which can increase its value, attract end-user and the marketers. This requires them to get involved in understanding the process, get involved, and use skills to reap benefits out of this.

SITUATION ANALYSIS/ PROBLEM STATEMENT

Millets are considered as miracle grains as they are the store houses of the nutrients required for healthy living. Malnutrition among vulnerable groups such as adolescent girls, pregnant and lactating women and children is an enduring problem in rural areas. Millets offer protentional solution for combating malnutrition as they are rich in carbohydrates, protein, minerals and phyto chemicals. Though they are the oldest grains since ages people have been consuming, their consumption have been gradually decreased due to difficulty of its processing and surplus availability of rice and wheat after green revolution. But processing and value addition of millets have lot of scope in promoting balanced diet and entrepreneurship.
